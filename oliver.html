Oliver
Oliver is an app designed to help people manage their daily medication regimen by sending reminders regarding their doses. It utilizes habit building techniques, like streaks, to help form better habits around their medication. It also provides potential side effects and medication interactions, ensuring users stay informed and never miss a dose.

(Student Project)
ROLE
UX/UI Designer
TIMELINE
OCT 2024 - NOV 2024

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Missing medication isn’t just inconvenient, for some, it can lead to serious health consequences. After seeing how delayed doses affected loved ones with Parkinson’s, I wanted to create a tool that helps people stay consistent with their meds in a way that feels natural and unobtrusive.

The low-fidelity wireframes outline a simple, habit-focused experience with screens for sign-up, daily reminders, streak tracking, profile settings, and pharmacy contact. Each design is intentionally minimal to help users build medication routines that feel effortless and integrated into daily life.

Branding

The branding is designed to feel trustworthy, friendly, and supportive, avoiding the cold, clinical tone of many existing apps. Instead, I focused on a warm, human-centered approach, using playful illustrations to create a more inviting experience.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

With the branding in place, the high-fidelity wireframes bring the experience to life. The screens now reflect the friendly, approachable tone of the brand, with soft colors and playful illustrations. I added encouraging notification pop-ups to help users stay motivated and continue meeting their goals. Small habit-building features, like linking medications to daily routines and noting where they’re stored, support lasting, consistent use.
